  Learning Spanish was a deeply immersive and rewarding experience
  and has given me the the ability to build international relationships
  and work with organisations I otherwise would not have. One of these
  organisastions is Mano Cambiada, an educational foundation which uses
  ecotourism to bring opportunities to rural communities in Colombia.
  Here I learned how impactful a well-organised and team-driven operation
  can be, but also how technology can be a limiting factor for growth and
  recognition.

  It is now clear to me that working with charities and companies for
  social-good is where I see myself in the future. Up until now I have
  contributed through administrative and teaching roles which has been
  a great experience. However going forward I realise my strengths lie
  in a more technical role, building elegant and robust software to better
  these sorts of organisations, this is where I can really make a
  significant impact.

  Much like learning another language, learning to code requires determination
  and complete emersion. I am serious about software and my goal of becoming
  a full-stack developer over the next few years will be my biggest challenge
  to date, but with or without FAC I am excited for the journey. Some
  months ago, FreeCodeCamp shone some light on the previously alien
  world of programming, then meeting with other beginners at FAC meet-ups
  gave me my first exposure to the friendly and supportive community.
  To attending a workshop run by NodeGirls where my confidence grew a
  little, the first few months has already been deeply rewarding.
